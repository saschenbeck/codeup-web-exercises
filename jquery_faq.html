What makes a park a National Park?

The National Park System and Congress determine if a park is to be a National Park
by verifying if the park has met a predetermined criteria, consisting of Natural Values
and Importance In History.

How many National Parks are there?

Currently, there are 419 parks that are recognized by both the National Park System
and Congress.

When did the country start designating the title of National Park?

The official start of the National Park System was created in 1916 by and act signed
by President Woodrow Wilson; However, the first National Park was Yellowstone National Park,
established by and act signed by President Ulysses S. Grant in 1872.

What is the largest National Park site?

The largest National Park is Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK at 13.2 million acres.

What is the smallest National Park site?

The smallest National Park is Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA at 0.02 acres.

How many people visit National Parks?

In 2019, there was a total of 327.5 million recreational visitors to the National Parks.

How much does it cost to enter a National Park?

Fewer than one-third of the national parks charge entrance fees. You can obtain park entrance passes
by visiting a park site that charges an entrance fee. Entrance fee sites have passes available;
we recommend calling a park prior to your visit. (See the park search to locate a specific park.)

How do I make reservations for camping/lodging in National Parks?

For campground reservations, visit Recreation.gov. Not all parks participate in this service;
many campgrounds are first come, first served. For more information on specific camping and lodging services
offered at the park(s) of your interest, please check specific park websites.

How do I apply for a job with the National Park Service?

National Park Service jobs, including both permanent and seasonal positions, are listed on USAJOBs.
